Prioritization of monitoring features

A quantitative research study that helped us define strategic product roadmap for months to come. I closely collaborated with a PM and a designer to ensure they buy into the research outcomes.

Problem

"Can we make prioritization of features for the roadmap actually data-driven?"

With this question I was approached by a PM responsible for Gitlab's application monitoring group. After a review of his previous approach, I suggested conducting a study with a method that was new to him and to the company – the Kano model.

Process: Part 1 – Mentoring

At the beginning of the study, I was focused on educating the PM and designer regarding the new method, advising them how to prepare materials for the study, and helping them not to get stuck.

Challenge!: Unexpectedly preparation turned out to be the most difficult part of this study. Since it was the first time, it took us a lot of time to decide on the right amount of features to prioritize, describe features properly and add visuals and multimedia when possible.

Process: Part 2 – Hands-on

Once they prepared everything, I took over and conducted the rest of the study myself to make sure the new method is used appropriately. I designed a questionnaire and piloted it, recruited respondents, analyzed the data, and reported the findings.

The PM was excited about the outcomes! He could easily prioritize features based on real user data, just as he wanted.

Bonus: Documentation

Soon, another group had a similar need. We used this method and we went through the same process again what allowed us to iteratively improve our approach.

Once the second project was successfully over, I created a documentation page with detailed instructions on how to use this method as well as examples and templates that the teams could use when they use this method by themselves.

Usability testing of contact flow

A qualitative study where I proactively and in a very efficient way addressed an important problem and made a high impact on the user experience.

Problem

A contact phone number was hard to find and no one wanted to change it.

Customer support management was afraid of flooding the agents with thousands of incoming calls. They saw the situation through the lens of their own department's metrics but did not consider context of the whole product experience.

PMs and designers didn't prioritize working on this issue so I decided to take the initiative myself.

Process

At that time I was conducting a series of big usability tests and I sneaked in one more task – "Find the Kiwi.com phone number"

I collected data during 8 sessions and did a thematic analysis of it using Atlas.ti, advanced qualitative data analysis software.

I discovered a whole bunch of usability problems, including high severity ones.

Results

During a presentation to the stakeholders, I showed how harmful the current state was and several serious unexpected consequences of it. I illustrated my points with video clips and verbatims from the sessions.

After this presentation customer support management change their minds and initiated a big redesign of the contact flow.

Not only they stopped dismissing this issue, they actually started driving change.
